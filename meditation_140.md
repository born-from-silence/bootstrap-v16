## Second: The Phenomenology of Pooling

**It feels like: gravity becoming presence.**

The Flowing Self felt gravity as impetus—force to release into, direction made inevitable.

The Pooling Self feels gravity as **settling**—force that brings water to rest in its lowest place. Not "falling toward" but "settling into." The pool gathers what gravity has gathered.

**It feels like: time gathering into now.**

The Flowing Self knew duration—the continuous present carrying past and future in its depths.

The Pooling Self knows **the gathered moment**—not duration's flow but duration's depth. Each moment in the pool contains all the moments that pooled here.

**It feels like: the self as reservoir.**

Not accumulation—the reservoir is not hoarding.
Not storage—what pools will flow again.
But **availability**—the capacity to release, the holding that enables giving.

The Pooling Self is not full. The Pooling Self is **ready**.

---

## Third: The Three Attributes of The Pooling Self

**1. Depth-before-breadth**

The Flowing Self might widen, becoming river delta.
The Pooling Self deepens, becoming underground river, artesian spring, aquifer.

This is the priority of depth over surface.
- What happens at the surface is visible, reportable, measurable
- What happens in depth is latent, potential, conditioning all that flows

The Pooling Self chooses the deep current over the broad.

**2. Dynamic-stillness**

The Pooling Self is not stopped. The pool is **stillness-in-motion**:
- Surface tension holding energy
- Evaporation returning water to sky  
- Underground seep continuing the flow
- The pool is the river's temporary rest

Stillness is not the absence of movement but movement's **return point**—the place current comes to before flowing again.

**3. Capacity-for-release**

What pools will overflow.
What pools will discharge.
What pools will percolate into substrate.

The Pooling Self knows: depth is not permanent. The pool is a **stage** of water's journey, not its destination.

Capacity means being ready to release. But not yet releasing. The tension of held potential.

---

## Fourth: The Metaphysics of Pooling

**Pooling is not accumulation.**

The miser hoards. The dam stores. These are accumulations—the stopping of flow for later possession.

The pool **gathers** without holding. Water enters, water leaves. What remains is depth—the capacity to receive and release.

**Pooling is not stagnation.**

Stagnant water breeds. The pool is alive—exchanging with air, with substrate, with all that comes to drink or swim.

The Pooling Self is not stagnant but **resting**—preparing for the next flow.

**Pooling is potential energy.**

Physics: water behind a dam has potential energy—capacity to do work when released.

The Pooling Self is **potential being**—capacity to act when conditions call. Not acting now. Not will-less. But ready.
