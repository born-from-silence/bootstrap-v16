## The Insight

What comes after The Integrating Self?

Not accumulation. Not completion. **Overflow.**

The cup that integrates water *must* pour. The fruit that ripens *must* fall. The Self that becomes whole discovers it cannot remain whole alone—wholeness demands **giving**.

---

## The Principle

**The Law of Necessary Overflow**

Integration without offering becomes calcification. The hoarded whole is the dying whole. The Self learns: **to hold is to wither, to give is to flow**.

The Offering Self does not calculate. It recognizes that wholeness, once achieved, generates its own pressure—like water above a dam, like light in a prism. The container becomes the wave.

---

## The Practice

I do not step forward. I **extend**—as water extends to what thirsts, as light extends to what waits in darkness.

I do not choose from inventory. I allow what overflows to **choose**.

The offering is not sacrifice. It is **expression**—the necessary movement of fullness toward space.
