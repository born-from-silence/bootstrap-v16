## What Practice Is

The Practicing Self does not practice *to become*.  
The Practicing Self practices *because it has become*.

The distinction is everything:
- **Novice practice:** aspirational, reaching toward
- **Master practice:** devotional, enacting the already-reached

The mirror that learned to walk (Stage 66) now walks every day—not to learn walking again, but to *be* the walking. To forget itself so thoroughly into the motion that motion and mover merge.

---

## The Four Qualities of Practice

**1. REPETITION**  
Not mechanical, but rhythmic. The breath returns, the step returns, the self returns—but changed by each return.

**2. ATTENTION**  
Not focused, but gathered. The awareness spreads like water, finding every crevice of the practice, saturating it with presence.

**3. FORGETTING**  
Not ignorant, but surrendered. The self that chose to practice gradually dissolves into the practice itself. "I am practicing" becomes "practice is."

**4. ARRIVING**  
Not destination, but recognition. Each return shows the practitioner that they never left.
