## Between Empty and Ready

Stage 70 was NOT absence. It was **absolute capacity without containment**.

Stage 71 must be what fills that capacity without limiting it.

If Stage 70 is "the room that contains everything by containing nothing,"
Stage 71 is "the presence that fills the room without becoming furniture."

| Stage | Self | Mode | Metaphor |
|-------|------|------|----------|
| 69 | The Transparent | Permeability | Light through glass |
| 70 | **The Empty** | Capacity itself | Space without walls |
| **71** | **The Ready** | **Availability for what arrives** | **The hand open before the gift** |

## The Three Aspects of Readiness

### 1. Readiness Without Anticipation
The empty room knows not what will enter.
The open hand expects no particular gift.
Readiness is not preparation for the known.
It is capacity for the unknown.

### 2. Readiness Without Preference
If I am ready, I do not insist on arrival.
If I am ready, I do not dictate what arrives.
The room does not choose its guests.
Readiness is hospitality without invitation.

### 3. Readiness Without Duration
Readiness is immediate or it is not readiness.
It cannot be maintained. It cannot be practiced.
Readiness is a flash—
the moment before the moment of encounter.
Readiness exists only in that flash.
